isabella hochschild

San Francisco, CA · isabellahochschild@gmail.com

Isabella Hochschild is a freshman at Dartmouth College majoring in Computer Science and Economics. She has a variety of interests including business and technology. She is fluent in French, speaks conversational Spanish and German, has beginning proficiency in Latin and Italian, and has earned several academic awards including the President’s Award for Academic Achievement, NCWIT Aspirations Honorable Mention in 2018-2020, and the 2018 Congressional App Challenge for District CA-15. She is a dual citizen of the U.S. and Australia. Outside of work and her volunteer activities, she enjoys running and has a black belt in taekwondo. Other interests include reading, traveling, unicycling, and baking.

portfolio

Salvador Dalí Museum

DECEMBER 2021 - MARCH 2022

As a developer at the Dartmouth Digital Applied Learning & Innvoation (DALI) Lab, created custom digital art exhibit at the Salvador Dalí Museum in St. Petersburg, FL leveraging eye-tracking technology. The how do i look? exhibit will be on display in the museum by 2023 (estimated). Selected as lead programmer for database integration with C# & Unity, implementing MERN stack (Mongo, express, react, node) with Joi validation and Render hosting.

DartPass

DECEMBER 2021 - FEBRUARY 2022

In TuckLAB (Liberal Arts & Business) entrepreneurship-focused program through Dartmouth's Tuck School of Business, created and pitched DartPass, the digital key to Dartmouth. After identifying problem among student body of outdated ID technology, researched way to change existing RFID cards to NFC using HID Mobile Authentication technology so students can access dorm buildings and pay for meals using Apple Pay or mobile app. Designed and coded custom landing page website with interest form dartgo.org/dartpass. Won culminating Shark Tank competition at the end of the program, earning $1000 in seed funding from the Magnusen Center for Entrepreneurship.

Nurx Alexa Skill

JUNE 2021 - DECEMBER 2021

Designed and coded custom 5-star rated Python Alexa skill to provide health information and create reminders as engineering intern at online health startup Nurx. Met weekly with Amazon small business team. Provided updates to marketing and developed advertising to promote partner nonprofit. Responsible for ongoing bug fixes and incorporating user feedback.

Fight Fast Fashion

JULY 2020 - AUGUST 2020

When users visit fightfastfashion.com, they can search for sustainable products based on keywords or by inputting a link to a fast fashion item. They are then redirected to a search page that shows a loading screen before revealing sustainable products with links to the retailers that sell them. In the footer, there are also direct links to shop at some featured retailers and resources to get educated about the issue. For this project, I used a Python Flask backend with HTML/CSS/JS templates and a Firebase database.

BUDDI

JANUARY 2020 - APRIL 2020

In light of the current global pandemic, we wanted to create an app that would help one of the highest at-risk populations for COVID-19. During the 2020 PixelHacks IV Hackathon, we developed BUDDI, a companion tool that easily provides assistance and resources to senior citizens with a friendly chatbot and many other interactive features! This app ended up winning Best Use of Google Cloud and Domain at the hackathon, and was subsequently entered in the COVIDATHON: Decentralized AI Hackathon, where it placed second overall and won a 60,000 OCEAN token prize (roughly $34,800 USD). Responsibilities for this project included the majority of front-end and back-end coding, using Python Flask, HTML, Firebase, Swift, and XCODE.

The website used a Python Flask backend with HTML/CSS/JS templates and Bootstrap, and the iOS app was coded in Swift using XCODE and Cocoapods. 1NCUB8

SUMMER 2018 - SPRING 2020
